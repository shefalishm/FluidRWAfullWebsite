## Real Estate Tokenization Is A Full Stack

Tokenized real estate is not just a token attached to a property. It requires a structure that connects the asset, legal ownership, investor rights, compliance rules, payments and reporting.

This is why vendor selection matters early. A weak stack can create problems during investor onboarding, transfer approvals, distributions or audits.

## Core Vendor Categories

A typical real estate tokenization project may need:

- legal and regulatory structuring
- tokenization platform
- KYC and investor eligibility checks
- transfer restriction and compliance tooling
- custody or wallet infrastructure
- fiat and stablecoin payment rails
- investor portal, reporting and communications
- distribution or marketplace support

Some platforms bundle several layers, but teams should still understand which responsibilities are handled internally and which require partners.

## Key Questions Before Vendor Selection

Start with jurisdiction, asset type, investor geography, minimum investment size, investor eligibility and expected liquidity. These choices affect the legal structure and the technology stack.

For example, a private real estate fund targeting accredited investors is different from a fractional ownership product marketed to retail users.
